Brighter Super Nuances & TPD Definitions [1]

Part A: Any Occupation (TPD Definition 1)

Applies to those working average 15+ hours/wk prior to date of disablement [1]. Must be unlikely to ever work in any occupation suited by education, training, experience, OR *may become reasonably suited* due to reasonable retraining or rehabilitation [1].

Part B: Any Occupation (TPD Definition 2)

Applies to those working <15 hours/wk or unemployed [1]. Unlikely ever to work in suited employment after considering education, training, experience [1].

TPD Age-Based Tapering Clause

Fixed-dollar cover for TPD reduces proportionally by 10% each year starting from age 62 (90%) down to age 70 (10%) when it fully terminates [1].

Income Protection Definition & Limits

Standard IP has a 104-week payment limit [1]. Extended IP can continue to age 65 [1]. Minimum hours worked to qualify is 14 hours per week [1]. Maximum IP weekly cap is $6,923.08/week [1].

Cbus Super Nuances & TPD Definitions [2]

Part 1: Unlikely to Return to Work

Applies if in work or unemployed <24 months [2]. Off work 3 consecutive months [2]. Must be unlikely ever to engage in regular paid work suited by education, training, or experience [2].
Favorable: No retraining penalty clause [2]

Part 2: Everyday Work Activities & Psychiatric Impairment

Applies if unemployed >24 months [2]. Must be unable to perform at least 2 of 5 everyday work activities (mobility, communication, vision, lifting, manual dexterity) [2], OR have a PIRS mental impairment score of 19%+ [2].

TPD Unit-Based Reduction (Tapering)

Cover declines annually because the value per unit declines dynamically as age increases [2] (e.g. at age 35 Manual unit is $33,800 [2], dropping to $12,000 at age 60 [2]).

Income Protection Definitions & Limits

2-year or 5-year benefit periods [2]. Must be working 15+ hours/wk to qualify [2]. Maximum IP benefit is calculated as the lesser of: 87% of pre-disability income (75% to member, 12% to super [2]), or amount of cover, or $30,000/month [2].

Sandbox Calculations Methodology:

Brighter Super: Evaluated using the standard Fixed-dollar tables (Page 13) [1]. Under Brighter Super rules, TPD and Death are packaged into a combined "Death & TPD" rate [1]. TPD cover is capped at or below the Death cover amount [1]. Cost calculations apply the mandatory 7% administration rebate [1].

Cbus Super: Cover in Cbus is inherently structured as unit multiples [2]. The sandbox dynamically calculates unit requirements by dividing your requested sums insured by Cbus's age-specific unit definitions (Pages 20-25) [2] and rounding up to the nearest whole unit, which matches standard voluntary insurance application protocols [2].
